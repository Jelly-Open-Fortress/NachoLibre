Nacho Libre

Released: 2006 • Directed by Jared Hess • Starring Jack Black

Synopsis

Ignacio (Jack Black) is a cook for the Oaxaca monastery orphanage in Mexico. While he cares deeply for the orphans, his food is terrible due to a lack of funds for quality ingredients. After being beaten up for tortilla chips by a local street thief named Esqueleto, Ignacio decides to secretly moonlights as a luchador wrestler to win prize money.

Forming a tag-team duo with Esqueleto, Ignacio takes on the ring name "Nacho". He must hide his forbidden wrestling identity from the monastery friars and the beautiful, devout Sister Encarnación, all while aiming to defeat the legendary champion, Ramses.

"When you are a man, sometimes you wear stretchy pants in your room. It's for fun." — Ignacio

Character Roster

  • Ignacio / Nacho: An eccentric monk-turned-wrestler fighting to give the orphans a better life (and buy better salad ingredients).
  • Steven / Esqueleto: Ignacio's skinny, chip-stealing tag-team partner who doesn't believe in God, only in "science".
  • Sister Encarnación: A kind, newly arrived nun at the orphanage who unknowingly captures Ignacio's heart.
  • Ramses: The reigning champion of lucha libre. Vain, ruthless, and wears a golden mask. He is completely uncharitable to fans and orphans alike.
  • Chancho: Ignacio's favorite orphan who uncovers Nacho's secret identity and helps keep his secret safe.

Production Metadata

Director Jared Hess (Director of *Napoleon Dynamite*)
Screenplay Jared Hess, Jerusha Hess, Mike White
Filming Location Oaxaca, Mexico
Based On The true story of Fray Tormenta (Sergio Gutiérrez Benítez), a real Mexican priest who wrestled for 23 years to fund his orphanage.
Soundtrack Highlights Features music by Danny Elfman and original songs sung by Jack Black.
